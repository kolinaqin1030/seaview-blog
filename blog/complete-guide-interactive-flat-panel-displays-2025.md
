## What is an Interactive Flat Panel (IFP)?

An interactive flat panel is a large-format, touch-enabled display with built-in compute and collaboration tools. Unlike projectors, there is no lamp to replace and no shadowing when someone stands in front of the screen. Unlike interactive whiteboards, an IFP is a single, integrated device that runs an operating system and common apps out of the box.

Typical use cases:
- K–12 classrooms for instruction and group work
- Higher education and corporate training
- Conference rooms and huddle spaces
- Digital signage or wayfinding in public areas

While vendors use different product names (for example, “ViewBoard” at ViewSonic or “SMART Board” at Smartboards.com), most share core components: a 4K LCD panel, touch system, integrated speakers, and an Android-based OS with EDLA certification on newer models. When an IFP is “EDLA-certified,” it means the device runs Google Mobile Services and has passed compatibility checks for Play Store access, account sign-in, and security policies.

## How IFPs Work: Core Technologies in 2025

Modern IFPs combine three pillars: visual clarity, touch precision, and compute/software integrations.

### Display and optical stack
- Resolution: 4K UHD (3840×2160) is now the standard for classrooms and conference rooms.
- Panel type: IPS and VA panels appear across size ranges. IPS typically offers wider viewing angles; VA can deliver higher native contrast.
- Brightness: Many commercial IFPs target 350–450 nits. For bright rooms or large formats, brighter panels reduce glare and washed-out content.
- Anti-glare and anti-fingerprint: Most displays ship with etched glass that reduces reflections and resists smudges during daily use.
